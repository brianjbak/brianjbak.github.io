About Me

Growing up, I took interest in the decision aspects of sports, curious about how to incorporate statistics to improve in-game strategies and make decisions driven by data. From then on, these interests growing up led me to Chapman University where I am expected to graduate this May with degrees in Data Analytics and Business Administration.

As I’ve progressed throughout my academic career, I’ve been exposed to a variety of other areas where analytics and data strategy can be applied, such as the business sector. This led me to pursue an internship with NASCAR, a sport and business I was unfamiliar with at the time, and granted me the opportunity to gain real world experience by applying what I’ve learned in school to the fields of consumer behavior, research & insights, and data strategy.

These lessons and experiences have greatly influenced what I look for in my career, and I'm hoping to pursue a role that reflects these sentiments.
